# Knowledge Management Assessment

## 1. Organization Profile

### 1.1 Company Overview

**Ethiopian Airlines Group (ETG)** is Africa's largest and most successful aviation enterprise, established in 1945. Headquartered at Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa, the airline operates as a state-owned enterprise while maintaining global competitiveness.

**Key Statistics:**
- 11 core functional departments including MRO, Flight Operations, Ethiopian Aviation University, and Skylight Hotel
- Pan-African network across secondary hubs in Togo, Malawi, and Zambia
- High-reliability organization with "Ethiopianization" as a core principle

### 1.2 Current Knowledge Management Culture

The KM culture at Ethiopian Airlines is a **hybrid system**, combining:

**Strengths:**
- **Learning Organization Characteristics**: Structured training programs, continuous professional development, Ethiopian Aviation University
- **Market-Driven Efficiency**: Strong performance orientation and operational excellence
- **Established Explicit Repositories**: Technical manuals, safety certifications through the Aviation University
- **Active Mentorship Culture**: Cross-functional workshops and structured mentorship programs

**Challenges:**
- Gap between management expectations and employee preferences for collaboration
- High-pressure operational environment limiting voluntary tacit knowledge sharing

## 2. Audit Findings

### 2.1 Institutional Strengths

| Strength | Evidence | Impact |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Balanced Scorecard Alignment** | "Learning and Growth" is a core BSC metric alongside financial targets | KM recognized at strategic level |
| **Incentivized Sharing Culture** | KPIs include knowledge contribution bonuses | Cultural acceptance of sharing |
| **Aviation University Infrastructure** | Standardized curriculum and certification programs | Strong explicit knowledge foundation |
| **Cross-functional Collaboration** | Structured workshops in Customer Service and Purchasing | Existing collaboration patterns |

### 2.2 Critical Challenges

#### Challenge 1: Knowledge Silos Across Departments

**Finding**: Knowledge sharing is limited within specific teams. Cross-departmental information exchange is not systematic, leading to duplication of effort.

**Impact**: 
- Flight Operations, Engineering, Customer Service, and Ground Operations operate independently
- 30+ minutes average time to find cross-functional information
- Repeated operational errors due to lack of lessons-learned sharing

#### Challenge 2: The "Volatile Sharing" Crisis

**Finding**: Significant knowledge exchange occurs via Microsoft Teams, Outlook, and Telegram. Knowledge is unstructured, buried in private inboxes, impossible to search or archive.

**Root Cause**: 
- No centralized tacit knowledge repository
- Volatile communication channels dominate over structured systems
- "Portal fatigue" from disconnected specialized tools

#### Challenge 3: Access Inequality

**Finding**: Management and administrative staff have ERP access, while frontline employees (technicians, ground staff, cabin crew) have limited access due to work nature.

**Impact**:
- Imbalance in knowledge availability
- Frontline staff lack mobile-accessible knowledge tools
- 6-month onboarding bottleneck for new hires

#### Challenge 4: Knowledge Loss Risk

**Finding**: No formal process to capture knowledge from retiring experts.

**Risk Areas**:
- Senior engineers with decades of troubleshooting experience
- Pilots with specialized weather approach knowledge
- Customer service leaders with "Spirit of Africa" hospitality expertise

## 3. Critical Knowledge Assets ("Crown Jewels")

### 3.1 Tacit MRO Troubleshooting Logic

**Type**: Technical Asset  
**Description**: Experiential diagnostic logic held by senior engineers for solving complex faults in unique African environmental conditions  
**Risk**: Brain drain from retiring experts  
**Protection Priority**: Critical

### 3.2 Multi-Hub Strategic Operational Logic

**Type**: Operational Asset  
**Description**: Institutional memory for managing pan-African network across secondary hubs, including regional aviation regulations and cross-border logistics  
**Risk**: Expansion without documented operational procedures  
**Protection Priority**: High

### 3.3 Aeronautical Institutional Memory

**Type**: Educational Asset  
**Description**: Both explicit (standardized curriculum) and tacit (teaching methods, safety culture) knowledge from Ethiopian Aviation University  
**Risk**: Instructor turnover without knowledge transfer  
**Protection Priority**: Medium-High

### 3.4 "Spirit of Africa" Hospitality DNA

**Type**: Cultural Asset  
**Description**: Tacit cultural intelligence including African passenger preferences, onboard coffee ceremony protocols, and regional linguistic nuances  
**Risk**: Cultural dilution during rapid expansion  
**Protection Priority**: High

## 4. User Needs Assessment

### 4.1 Core User Personas

#### Persona: Senior Pilot (Expert)
- **Profile**: Captain with 15+ years experience, 10,000+ flight hours
- **Needs**: Share knowledge about challenging weather approaches, mentor junior pilots
- **Pain Points**: No structured way to document experiential knowledge

#### Persona: Junior Maintenance Technician
- **Profile**: Recent hire, mobile-first user, works in hangar environment
- **Needs**: Quick access to troubleshooting guides, expert consultation
- **Pain Points**: Manuals hard to find, must wait for senior coach availability

#### Persona: Ground Staff (Regional Airport)
- **Profile**: Works at Bahir Dar or Dire Dawa with unreliable connectivity
- **Needs**: Offline access to passenger handling procedures
- **Pain Points**: Limited internet, no mobile-accessible knowledge tools

#### Persona: Innovation Committee Member
- **Profile**: Manager reviewing improvement ideas
- **Needs**: Dashboard view of submitted ideas, voting mechanism
- **Pain Points**: Ideas scattered across emails, no tracking system

### 4.2 Functional Requirements

| Requirement | Priority | User Group |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| Full-text search across all content | Critical | All |
| Mobile-responsive design | Critical | Frontline staff |
| Offline content access | High | Regional stations |
| Expert directory with availability | Critical | Junior staff |
| Video/audio knowledge capture | High | Experts |
| Idea submission and voting | Medium | All |
| Multilingual support (Amharic) | High | Ground operations |
| SOP acknowledgment tracking | Critical | Compliance |

## 5. Current Systems Analysis

### 5.1 Existing Tools

| System | Purpose | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|---------|-----------|------------|
| **SAP** | ERP, financial data | Robust data management | Limited to admin staff |
| **Maintenix** | Maintenance records | Technical documentation | Isolated from other departments |
| **MS Teams/Outlook** | Communication | Widely adopted | Volatile, unsearchable |
| **Telegram** | Informal sharing | Mobile-friendly | Unstructured, no governance |
| **Aviation University LMS** | Training content | Standardized curriculum | Limited to trainees |

### 5.2 Integration Points

The EAKP will **complement** (not replace) existing systems:
- Link to SAP for HR data (read-only)
- Reference Maintenix for technical specifications
- Replace Teams/Outlook for structured knowledge sharing
- Integrate with Aviation University content
